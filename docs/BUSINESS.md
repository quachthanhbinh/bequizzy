## 1. Market Opportunity

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
The global sales automation and engagement software market is estimated at **$7.8B in 2026**, growing at ~14% CAGR. The broader CRM + sales intelligence market exceeds **$60B**.

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
RevLooper targets solo operators and teams of 1–10 in the SMB segment:
- **Global:** ~120 million small businesses with B2B sales needs
- **Southeast Asia (Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia, Philippines):** ~15 million SMEs, largely underserved by English-first tools
- Content-driven operators (travel, insurance, recruitment, marketing) who generate leads from social content but have no conversion system
- Estimated SAM: **$1.5B** (SMB AI outreach, content-driven inbound, and light CRM tools)

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — Year 1
Target: 2,000 paying customers at average $55/mo = **$1.32M ARR**

### Why Now
1. **LLM cost collapse:** GPT-4o-mini costs <$0.001/email — AI outreach is now economically viable at $39/mo price points
2. **SMB digital adoption:** Post-pandemic, SEA SMBs are actively adopting digital sales tools
3. **Content-to-revenue gap:** Millions of SEA operators post on Facebook/TikTok daily but have no system to capture, follow up, or convert that engagement into revenue
4. **Tool consolidation:** Solo founders are overwhelmed by having Apollo + Instantly + ManyChat + Calendly + HubSpot stacked; an all-in-one wins

### Competitor Strengths to Respect
- **Apollo.io:** Massive contact database (600M+). RevLooper does not compete here — RevLooper helps users reach their own leads, not Apollo's database.
- **Instantly.ai:** Unlimited email account stacking is a strong feature for high-volume cold emailers. RevLooper's response: quality over quantity — AI personalization beats volume.
- **HubSpot:** Brand trust and ecosystem depth. RevLooper's response: 10-minute setup vs HubSpot's week-long onboarding.
- **ManyChat / Chatfuel:** Chatbot-based comment DM automation. RevLooper's response: RevLooper goes beyond keyword DMs — it creates a full lead profile, enriches it, routes it into a campaign, and tracks the full conversion to revenue.

### RevLooper's Unfair Advantages
1. **Full revenue lifecycle** — the only tool connecting content-driven inbound + outbound outreach + pipeline + post-sale in one system
2. **Content-Driven Inbound Engine** — captures leads from Facebook/TikTok comments automatically; no Western competitor is building this for SEA operators
3. **SEA-first channel support** — Zalo + Facebook Messenger automation; no Western competitor serves this market
4. **Price-to-AI ratio** — GPT-4o + Claude quality at $39/mo; 11x.ai charges enterprise rates for same capability
5. **Chat-first campaign builder** — non-technical users build campaigns by talking, not configuring
6. **Vertical playbooks** — day-1 value for Recruitment, Travel, Insurance without any setup
7. **Multi-agent AI workforce (Wave 4)** — workspace owners rent specialist AI Employees (Marketing, Growth, Sales, Support) that all share the same AI Brain for business context; they operate as a complete AI-powered business. 11x.ai deploys a single SDR persona; RevLooper becomes a full multi-agent OS. No Western competitor offers this at $39–$149/mo price points.

### Credit System Design

**What are credits?**
Credits are the unit of AI consumption. They reset monthly on the billing date and do not roll over.

**Credit costs:**
| Operation | Credits |
|---|---|
| Generate email step | 1 |
| Regenerate / refine email | 2 |
| Generate full campaign (chat) | 5 |
| AI reply suggestion (3 options) | 2 |
| Lead enrichment | 3 |
| Meeting brief | 5 |

**Credit top-up packs** (available on all paid plans):
| Pack | Credits | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | 500 | $5 |
| Growth | 2,500 | $20 |
| Power | 10,000 | $60 |

**Why credits instead of per-seat pricing?**
Solo users and micro-teams have *variable* monthly activity — a recruiter may send 1,000 emails one month and 50 the next. Per-seat pricing penalizes irregular usage. Credits align cost with actual value consumed.

## 5. Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1 GTM: Vietnam-first, English Product

**Why Vietnam first?**
- Founder has local network and business development capability
- 700,000+ SMEs and independent professionals actively seeking sales tools
- Zalo (90M users in Vietnam) is a unique SEA channel no competitor serves
- Lower CAC due to direct founder-led sales and community relationships
- Vietnam success builds social proof for Southeast Asia expansion

**Target verticals in Vietnam:**
1. Recruitment agencies and freelance recruiters (large, active community)
2. Insurance agents (Prudential, Manulife — large agent networks)
3. Real estate agents (expanding into this in Phase 2)
4. Digital marketing agencies

**English product, Vietnamese support:**
- Product UI in English (global-ready from day 1)
- Customer support available in Vietnamese
- Local payment methods: MoMo, VNPay, bank transfer (in addition to Stripe)
- Vietnamese onboarding content and playbooks

### Distribution Channels

#### Channel 1 — Direct Outreach (Ironically)
- Use RevLooper itself to do outbound prospecting to recruitment agencies, insurance networks
- Dogfooding as a demo tool
- Target: 50 paying customers in first 3 months

#### Channel 2 — Community-Led Growth
- Vietnam tech/startup communities: Topcv, ITViec, Vietnam Startups Facebook groups
- LinkedIn content: "I'm building an AI sales tool in public" (build in public strategy)
- Twitter/X: Technical audience, product building content
- Target: 1,000 free signups in 3 months

#### Channel 3 — Vertical Partnerships
- Recruitment: partner with job boards (TopCV, ITViec) for co-promotion
- Insurance: partner with insurance training groups and MLM uplines
- Travel: partner with travel agent associations (VNAT)
- Target: 3 vertical partnerships in 6 months

#### Channel 4 — Content SEO (Month 3+)
- Target keywords: "AI cold email tool", "Zalo outreach automation", "recruitment automation Vietnam"
- Comparison pages: "RevLooper vs Apollo", "RevLooper vs Instantly"
- Playbook content: "How to automate recruitment outreach" (vertical-specific guides)
- Target: 500 organic signups/month by Month 6

#### Channel 5 — Product-Led Growth
- Free plan as viral acquisition: RevLooper footer branding on all free-plan emails
- Booking page at `revlooper.com/book/...` — visible to all prospects who receive a booking link
- Referral program: "Give 1 month Pro free, get 1 month Pro free" (Month 4+)

## 6. Customer Acquisition

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Targets
| Channel | Target CAC |
|---|---|
| Direct / founder outreach | $0 (time only) |
| Community (organic) | $0–$10 |
| Content / SEO | $15–$30 |
| Paid ads (Month 6+) | $40–$60 |
| Partnerships | $20–$40 |

### Onboarding Funnel
```
Sign up (free)
  → Onboarding wizard (industry, goal, ICP)
  → Connect email account
  → Import 10 leads (or use sample leads)
  → AI builds first campaign (chat)
  → Send first email
  → See reply in inbox
  → Book first meeting
  → [Hit credit/lead limit]
  → Upgrade prompt → Pro
```

**Activation metric:** User sends first email within 24 hours of signup.  
**Target activation rate:** ≥ 60%

### Retention Strategy
- **Week 1:** Onboarding email sequence (3 emails: "Set up done?", "First reply?", "Book your first meeting")
- **Week 2:** Product tips: "Did you know? AI can generate follow-ups automatically"
- **Month 1:** Monthly performance summary email ("You sent X emails, got Y replies, booked Z meetings")
- **At risk:** If user hasn't logged in for 7 days → email with "Your campaign is paused, here's what's waiting"
- **Churn prevention:** Credit balance low → notification with top-up option before credits hit 0

## 7. Revenue Model & Unit Economics

### Revenue Streams
1. **Subscription revenue** — recurring monthly/annual plan fees (primary)
2. **Credit top-ups** — one-time credit pack purchases (secondary)
3. **White-label add-on** — $50/month for Agency plan branding removal on booking pages
4. **AI Employee rental revenue** — flat monthly Paddle subscription per agent rented (prepaid, agent-license fee only; per-plan gate: Business+ required) plus metered per-token LLM cost billed separately to workspace credits at 30% margin. Each rented agent is an additive monthly line item on top of the base plan. ARPU impact: +$30–$150/mo per workspace as catalog grows.
5. **Partner referral fees** — Phase 4: loan marketplace referral commissions (5–10% of funded amount)

### Unit Economics Targets (Year 1)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) | $55/month (base plan); projected $80–$120/month post-AI-Employee launch |
| Monthly Churn | < 5% |
| LTV (at 5% monthly churn) | ~$1,100 (base); ~$1,800 post-AI-Employee |
| CAC (blended) | $40 |
| LTV:CAC Ratio | > 25:1 |
| Gross Margin | ~80% (SaaS margins minus AI API costs) |

### Enrichment Unit Economics

| Layer | Cost | RevLooper pricing | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hunter.io email verify (auto on import) | ~$0.001/verification (included in plan) | Free to user | Built into plan cost |
| Apollo.io B2B enrichment (on-demand) | ~$0.005/record (Basic plan) | 3 credits = ~$0.059 at Pro | ~91% margin per enrichment |
| At scale (100 workspaces × 1,000 enrichments/mo) | $500 Apollo cost | $5,900 revenue from credits | ~91% margin; volume discounts available at 100k+/mo |

### AI Cost Management
AI API costs are the main variable cost. Target: AI costs ≤ 10% of subscription revenue.

## 8. Growth Strategy

### Phase 1 — Traction (Months 0–6)
**Goal:** 300 paying customers, $15K MRR, product-market fit signal  
**Focus:** Manual sales, community outreach, zero CAC growth  
**Key actions:**
- Use RevLooper itself to do outbound for RevLooper (recruitment agencies in Vietnam)
- Build in public (LinkedIn + Twitter): weekly product updates, behind-the-scenes
- Personal outreach to 500 potential users in target verticals
- Launch on Product Hunt (Month 3)

### Phase 2 — Scale (Months 6–12)
**Goal:** 1,200 paying customers, $66K MRR  
**Focus:** SEO flywheel + partnership channels  
**Key actions:**
- 50+ SEO-optimized articles targeting sales automation + vertical keywords
- 3+ vertical partnerships (recruitment associations, insurance networks)
- Referral program launch
- Paid acquisition testing (Facebook/LinkedIn ads, $2K/mo budget)
- First YouTube tutorial content (targeting Vietnamese small business owners)

### Phase 3 — Expansion (Months 12–24)
**Goal:** $250K MRR, SEA expansion (Thailand, Indonesia)  
**Focus:** Geographic expansion + enterprise SMB upmarket  
**Key actions:**
- Thai and Indonesian market entry (localized playbooks + local payment methods)
- Self-serve API tier for agencies to build on RevLooper
- Strategic partnership with Vietnam banks for Phase 4 financial layer groundwork
- Consider a small seed round to fund growth marketing at this stage
