# BeQuizzy — Business Strategy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-19

---

## 1. Positioning

### Positioning Statement
**For SMEs hiring sales and business development talent, and for candidates trying to prove they can sell, communicate, and learn fast, BeQuizzy is the AI-native assessment and growth platform that turns soft-skill-heavy talent into trusted hiring evidence.**

Unlike job boards, CV filters, or one-time generic tests, BeQuizzy combines:
- realistic practice
- structured scoring
- visible improvement over time
- employer-facing evidence profiles

### Category
BeQuizzy sits at the intersection of:
- hiring assessment
- career upskilling
- talent marketplace infrastructure

The long-term opportunity is not “another test platform.” It is a **continuous talent evidence network**.

---

## 2. Why Now

### Market shift
The job market increasingly values:
- evidence over CV claims
- skills over pedigree
- coachability over static credentials
- repeatable hiring signals over gut feel

### Why non-technical commercial roles are attractive
Technical hiring has stronger benchmark systems already. Non-technical roles still rely heavily on:
- unstructured interviews
- personality impressions
- inconsistent screening
- weak early-career signals

That makes Sales/BD a strong wedge for BeQuizzy.

### Why candidate behavior matters
Most employer-side assessment tools are transactional. Candidates do not “live” there. BeQuizzy can build stronger long-term defensibility if candidates return weekly to practice, improve, and maintain a living profile.

## 4. Core Differentiation

### 4.1 Not just a test library
Legacy assessment tools often feel like one-time exams. BeQuizzy must feel like:
- a practice environment for candidates
- an evidence system for employers

### 4.2 Not just a job board
Job boards distribute demand but do not create trusted signal quality. BeQuizzy creates a richer hiring graph because the platform sees:
- communication behavior
- scenario judgment
- persuasion skill
- learning velocity
- consistency over time

### 4.3 Not just interview coaching
Interview coaching products help the candidate privately. BeQuizzy can turn improvement into a market-facing asset through the verified evidence profile.

### 4.4 The moat
The moat is not content alone. The moat is:
- repeated candidate interaction
- multimodal behavioral data
- score calibration by role family
- visible progress history
- employer usage data linked back to hiring outcomes

## 5. Competitive Landscape

BeQuizzy overlaps with multiple categories, but does not fit neatly into one existing bucket.

### Category A — Assessment platforms
Examples: TestGorilla, Criteria Corp, Vervoe, Toggl Hire, HireVue, myInterview, VidCruiter

**Their strength:** employer workflow and structured testing  
**Their weakness:** weak candidate habit loop, limited ongoing growth story, often transactional and company-owned

### Category B — Technical skill platforms
Examples: HackerRank, Codility, CodeSignal, Byteboard, Karat

**Their strength:** credible benchmark culture  
**Their weakness:** mostly technical-first; weaker fit for soft-skill-heavy commercial roles

### Category C — Talent networks / marketplaces
Examples: Toptal, Turing, Andela, Braintrust, Mercor, Micro1

**Their strength:** supply aggregation and recruiter access  
**Their weakness:** candidate-side daily utility is often limited; many are recruiter-first or expert-talent-first

### Category D — Sourcing / recruiting infrastructure
Examples: Gem, HireEZ, SeekOut, Eightfold, Beamery, Ashby

**Their strength:** sourcing workflow and recruiting ops  
**Their weakness:** depend heavily on existing profile data; they do not generate proprietary skill evidence through repeated practice

### BeQuizzy’s strategic gap to exploit
Most competitors optimize one of these:
- workflow
- database
- assessment delivery
- marketplace access

BeQuizzy should optimize a rarer combination:
- repeated candidate engagement
- multimodal evidence creation
- employer trust in soft-skill-heavy roles

## 6. Business Model

### Revenue stream 1 — Employer subscriptions
Primary early revenue source.

Target buyers:
- SMEs hiring sales/BD internally
- hiring managers
- team leads
- founders

Value sold:
- shortlist quality
- screening efficiency
- reduced hiring noise

### Revenue stream 2 — Candidate premium
Not the first revenue engine, but important later.

Candidate premium can include:
- advanced mock interviews
- deeper score diagnostics
- premium practice paths
- profile boost / verified highlight
- coach mode and interview prep packs

### Revenue stream 3 — Recruiter / agency access
Phase 2+, once the candidate pool and evidence layer become attractive enough.

## 7. Zero-to-One Go-to-Market

### 7.1 Candidate acquisition from zero
Primary channels:
- TikTok short-form interview content
- Facebook and LinkedIn content around sales interview prep
- job communities and career groups
- referral loops from candidates

Content themes:
- common sales interview mistakes
- objection handling drills
- “how recruiters actually evaluate junior sales talent”
- roleplay clips
- before/after improvement stories

### 7.2 Employer acquisition from zero
Primary channels:
- founder-led outbound to SMEs hiring salespeople
- direct demos with hiring managers
- proof from early candidate scorecards and pilot placements
- case studies around reduced screening time

### 7.3 Pilot strategy
The fastest way to prove value is not massive scale first. It is:
- 5–10 SME hiring pilots
- a focused candidate supply pool
- manually monitored match outcomes
- fast iteration on rubrics and score explanations

## 8. Retention Strategy

### Candidate retention
The product must create a weekly habit.

Core loop:
1. take a short module
2. receive detailed scorecard
3. see exact weakness
4. get next drill
5. return to improve score and profile

Retention drivers:
- visible progress trend
- goal-based training path
- practical realism
- recruiter visibility upside

### Employer retention
Employers return when the platform becomes part of real hiring motion.

Retention drivers:
- repeat hiring needs
- confidence in score interpretation
- strong shortlist outcomes
- faster hiring team alignment

## 9. Strategic Risks

### Risk 1 — Too broad too early
If BeQuizzy tries to assess all non-technical roles at once, it becomes generic.

**Mitigation:** start with Sales/BD only.

### Risk 2 — Low trust in AI scoring
If the score feels black-box or arbitrary, neither candidate nor employer will trust it.

**Mitigation:** use explainable rubrics, evidence snippets, and constrained role-specific competency models.

### Risk 3 — Strong candidate acquisition but weak employer demand
A candidate-only product can become coaching content without hiring outcomes.

**Mitigation:** run employer pilots early and measure interview conversion.

### Risk 4 — Strong employer demand but weak candidate retention
An employer-only assessment tool is easier to copy and weaker as a network.

**Mitigation:** keep candidate utility central, not secondary.
