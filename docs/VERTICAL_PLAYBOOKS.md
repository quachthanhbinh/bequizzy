# BeQuizzy — Role Playbooks

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-19

> These playbooks serve two purposes:
> 1. define how BeQuizzy evaluates each role family
> 2. provide structured source material for future AI scoring, coaching, and matching systems

---

## 1. Playbook Structure

Each role playbook should define:
- target roles
- core competencies
- assessment modules
- scoring logic
- evidence signals employers should see
- coaching recommendations candidates should receive

BeQuizzy should expand by **role family playbooks**, not by generic tests.

---

## 2. Beachhead Playbook — Sales / Business Development

### 2.1 Why this playbook comes first
Sales/BD is a strong wedge because hiring success depends on capabilities that CVs do not measure well:
- communication
- persuasion
- confidence under pressure
- objection handling
- situational judgment
- coachability

### 2.2 Example target roles
- Sales Development Representative (SDR)
- Business Development Executive
- Telesales Executive
- Junior Account Executive
- Inside Sales Representative

### 2.3 Core competency model
#### A. Communication clarity
Can the candidate explain clearly, structure thoughts, and sound easy to follow?

#### B. Persuasion and value framing
Can the candidate position an offer in a way that sounds relevant and convincing?

#### C. Objection handling
Can the candidate respond calmly and intelligently when challenged?

#### D. Situational judgment
Can the candidate choose sensible next actions in messy real-world scenarios?

#### E. Presentation ability
Can the candidate present a short pitch with structure, confidence, and coherence?

#### F. Coachability / growth velocity
Does the candidate adapt after feedback and improve quickly?

## 3. Sales/BD Assessment Modules

### 3.1 AI roleplay call
#### Purpose
Test live communication, listening, value framing, and objection handling.

#### Sample scenarios
- first recruiter screening for an SDR role
- discovery call with a skeptical prospect
- follow-up conversation after weak initial interest
- pricing objection from a prospect

#### Signals to score
- opening quality
- question quality
- listening relevance
- confidence and pacing
- objection response quality
- ability to move to a next step

### 3.2 Situational judgment test
#### Purpose
Test judgment and decision quality in commercial scenarios.

#### Sample scenarios
- prospect stops replying after initial interest
- lead says the price is too high
- candidate has limited product information
- hiring manager asks how to prioritize leads

#### Signals to score
- practical reasoning
- prioritization
- customer awareness
- professionalism
- risk awareness

### 3.3 Presentation / pitch module
#### Purpose
Test whether the candidate can frame, explain, and persuade in a short presentation.

#### Sample prompts
- pitch a simple software tool to an SME owner
- explain why a customer should take a follow-up call
- present a short market opportunity summary

#### Signals to score
- structure
- relevance
- confidence
- clarity
- closing strength

### 3.4 Communication layer
This layer cuts across all modules.

#### Signals to score
- spoken clarity
- fluency
- confidence
- response relevance
- English communication when required

## 4. Scorecard Design for Sales/BD

### Candidate-facing scorecard should answer
- What did I do well?
- Where did I lose points?
- What should I practice next?
- Am I improving over time?

### Employer-facing scorecard should answer
- Is this candidate interview-ready?
- Where are the strongest signals?
- What are the biggest risks?
- Is this person coachable enough to justify hiring as a junior?

### Example scorecard dimensions
- Communication: 0–100
- Objection Handling: 0–100
- Situational Judgment: 0–100
- Presentation: 0–100
- Growth Velocity: low / medium / high
- Readiness Level: not ready / developing / interview-ready

---

## 5. Evidence Profile Design

The Sales/BD profile should show more than a score.

### Core profile blocks
- target role
- completed modules
- current competency scores
- progress trend over time
- highlighted strengths
- development areas
- selected evidence snippets
- recommended interview focus areas

### Employer interpretation examples
- **High communication + low objection handling** → candidate may need coaching but can interview well
- **Medium readiness + high growth velocity** → strong junior potential
- **Strong situational judgment + weak presentation** → likely better in internal or support-heavy sales motions initially

---

## 6. Coaching Logic for Sales/BD

### Candidate improvement recommendations should be specific
Bad recommendation:
- “Improve your communication.”

Good recommendation:
- “Your answers were clear, but you missed the chance to ask discovery questions before pitching. Practice a 3-question discovery opener next.”

### Example drill mapping
- weak opening confidence → short warm-up roleplay drills
- weak objection handling → objection-specific practice packs
- weak presentation structure → 1-minute and 3-minute pitch drills
- weak situational judgment → scenario ladder exercises

---

## 8. Guardrails

### Do not do this
- generic mindset scoring with vague explanations
- personality labels without observable evidence
- “soft skills” scores that cannot be defended
- broad role coverage without role-specific rubrics

### Do this instead
- observable behavior
- role-specific scenario design
- explainable scoring
- repeated measurement over time

---

## 9. Strategic Use of Playbooks

Playbooks should become BeQuizzy’s internal operating system for expansion.

Every new vertical should only launch when BeQuizzy can answer clearly:
- what does good look like in this role?
- how do we simulate it?
- how do we score it?
- how does the employer use that output in hiring?
