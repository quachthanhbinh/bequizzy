# BeQuizzy — Product Roadmap

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-19

---

## Roadmap Summary

| Phase | Timeline | Focus | Core Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 0** | Weeks 0–6 | Wedge validation | Prove Sales/BD assessment value with pilot candidates and SMEs |
| **Phase 1** | Months 0–3 | Candidate habit loop | Launch modular practice + scorecards + evidence profile |
| **Phase 2** | Months 3–6 | Employer hiring workflow | Shortlist, compare, and use evidence in real hiring |
| **Phase 3** | Months 6–12 | Marketplace density | Build repeat matching loops and candidate premium |
| **Phase 4** | Months 12+ | Expansion | Extend to adjacent non-technical job families |

---

## Phase 0 — Wedge Validation (Weeks 0–6)

### Objective
Validate that SMEs hiring sales talent care about structured evidence, and that candidates will repeatedly practice to improve.

### Deliverables
- narrow role definition for v1: Sales / Business Development
- competency rubric v1
- 3 core module prototypes:
  - AI roleplay call
  - situational judgment
  - presentation / pitch
- pilot scorecard format
- early evidence profile prototype
- 5–10 SME pilot employers
- 50–100 pilot candidates

### Success signals
- candidates complete at least 2 modules
- employers prefer BeQuizzy evidence over CV-only screening
- at least some candidates are shortlisted or interviewed because of assessment output

---

## Phase 1 — Candidate Habit Loop (Months 0–3)

### Objective
Ship the first candidate product that creates repeat weekly usage.

### Core features
#### 1. Candidate onboarding
- choose target role
- choose seniority target
- define job goal

#### 2. Modular multimodal assessments
- voice
- video
- text
- mixed tasks

#### 3. Signature module set
- AI roleplay call
- situational test
- presentation / pitch test
- communication scoring layer

#### 4. AI scorecards
- competency breakdown
- strengths
- weaknesses
- improvement notes
- next recommended drill

#### 5. Verified Evidence Profile
- latest scores
- trend lines
- completed modules
- proof snippets
- role readiness summary

### Product goal
A candidate should finish a module, understand their gaps instantly, and want to come back.

### Phase 1 success metrics
- first module completion rate
- 7-day return rate
- % of candidates completing 3+ modules
- % of candidates improving on a tracked competency

---

## Phase 2 — Employer Hiring Workflow (Months 3–6)

### Objective
Turn candidate evidence into an employer product with clear ROI.

### Core features
#### 1. Employer account and role setup
- define target hiring profile
- create role-specific shortlist criteria

#### 2. Candidate discovery and shortlist
- rank candidates by role-fit
- filter by competency and readiness
- surface junior potential signals

#### 3. Evidence-first review
- competency explanations
- score reasoning
- evidence excerpts
- risk flags

#### 4. Candidate comparison
- compare finalists side by side
- identify trade-offs between readiness and growth potential

#### 5. Lightweight workflow
- save shortlist
- notes
- decision support for interviewers

### Product goal
An SME should be able to reduce weak interviews and improve shortlist quality.

### Phase 2 success metrics
- employer repeat usage
- time-to-shortlist reduction
- interview request rate from platform shortlist
- % of hiring decisions influenced by scorecards

---

## Phase 3 — Marketplace Density (Months 6–12)

### Objective
Connect candidate-side repetition with employer-side demand so the product becomes a real hiring ecosystem.

### Core features
#### 1. Candidate visibility controls
- verified profile sharing
- recruiter discoverability
- optional profile boosting

#### 2. Match engine
- job-fit ranking
- readiness plus potential views
- role-family matching improvements

#### 3. Candidate premium
- advanced mock interviews
- deeper diagnostics
- premium practice paths
- premium visibility

#### 4. Employer expansion
- recruiter / agency access
- reusable hiring templates
- team collaboration for shortlist review

### Product goal
Make BeQuizzy a place where candidates stay to improve and employers return to hire.

### Phase 3 success metrics
- active employer accounts
- interview requests per verified profile
- first paid candidate premium conversions
- match-to-placement conversion

---

## Phase 4 — Expansion (Months 12+)

### Objective
Expand carefully by adjacent job families, not by becoming a generic testing marketplace.

### Expansion order candidates
1. Customer Support / Customer Success
2. Recruiter / Talent Acquisition
3. Marketing / Growth / Content
4. Account Management / Operations-heavy commercial roles

### Rule for expansion
Each new job family needs:
- its own competency model
- its own scoring rubric
- its own scenario library
- its own employer value proposition

---

## Roadmap Principles

### 1. Wedge before breadth
Do not expand beyond Sales/BD before trust is strong.

### 2. Evidence before marketplace hype
A marketplace without trusted signal quality is fragile.

### 3. Retention before aggressive monetization
The candidate loop is a strategic asset, not just an acquisition funnel.

### 4. Explainability over black-box AI
Scorecards must be understandable and defensible.

---

## What not to build too early
- generic personality testing suite
- enterprise ATS replacement
- broad university admin workflows
- all job families at once
- giant public talent marketplace before signal trust exists
